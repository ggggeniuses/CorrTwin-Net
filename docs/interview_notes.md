# Interview Notes

## 30-Second Introduction

CorrTwin-Net is an AI + wireless communication project focused on fast prediction of wireless channel correlation functions. Instead of doing common CSI estimation or neural receiver design, it learns the mapping from communication scenario parameters to Temporal ACF, Spatial CCF, and Frequency FCF curves. The project includes theory sanity checks, MLP/ResMLP models, traditional ML baselines, OOD generalization, speed comparison, tests, and reproducible experiment scripts.

## 3-Minute Explanation

1. Many AI + communication projects focus on CSI estimation, OFDM detection, or neural receivers.
2. CorrTwin-Net chooses a narrower channel-modeling problem: surrogate prediction of ACF/CCF/FCF correlation curves.
3. The dataset is generated from a lightweight Rayleigh/Rician multipath simulator.
4. Inputs include carrier frequency, velocity, path count, Rician K-factor, antenna spacing, delay spread, and bandwidth; SNR is not part of the clean-channel statistical target.
5. Outputs are fixed-length ACF, CCF, and FCF curve samples.
6. MLP and ResMLP models are compared with Ridge, RandomForest, and KNN baselines.
7. The project evaluates random-test accuracy, theory sanity checks, OOD generalization, and inference speed.
8. The current simulator is lightweight and can be replaced by RIS-V2V GBSM/BDCM, Sionna, DeepMIMO, or ray-tracing data in future work.

## Common Questions

### Why not do CSI estimation?

CSI estimation and neural receiver projects already have many mature open-source examples. CorrTwin-Net focuses on wireless channel statistical properties, especially ACF, CCF, and FCF, which are useful for channel modeling and parameter sensitivity analysis.

### What do ACF, CCF, and FCF mean?

ACF describes temporal channel correlation and is closely related to mobility and Doppler shift. CCF describes spatial correlation across antenna positions or spacing. FCF describes frequency-domain correlation and is related to multipath delay spread and bandwidth.

### What is the practical value of the surrogate model?

It does not replace a standard channel model. It accelerates parameter sweeps after offline training, making it useful for early-stage sensitivity analysis and fast exploration of communication scenarios.

### Why include Ridge, RandomForest, and KNN?

Baselines prevent the project from becoming a deep-learning-only comparison. They show whether neural models are truly useful and where simpler regression methods are competitive.

### What does OOD generalization show?

OOD tests evaluate model behavior under unseen parameter ranges, such as higher velocity, stronger Rician K-factor, larger delay spread, or wider bandwidth. This is closer to research evaluation than only reporting random test-set error.

### What are the limitations?

The current simulator is lightweight and simulation-generated. It is not a substitute for 3GPP, GBSM, BDCM, measurement, or ray-tracing datasets. The project demonstrates a reproducible surrogate-modeling pipeline that can later use richer channel backends.

### How does this relate to RIS-V2V work?

RIS-V2V channel modeling can provide a more realistic label generator. CorrTwin-Net can then learn surrogate mappings for the ACF/CCF/FCF curves generated by that richer simulator.
